6. **P6.** Do not publish immediately after drafting. First quiz the user on granularity and blocking edges; that settles slice size and what truly blocks what. This differs from `to-spec`, which does not interview. Citation: `skills/to-tickets/reference/slicing.md`: “Unlike `to-spec`, which is pure synthesis and never interviews, **to-tickets interviews.**”

7. **P7.** Create A first, then B and C after A in either order, because A is the blocker and B/C are independent. If A is created as `#N`, B carries exactly `- [ ] depends on #N` in its ticket body, and C carries exactly `- [ ] depends on #N` in its ticket body. `backlog run` skips B and C while that unchecked, unclosed dependency blocks them. Citation: `skills/to-tickets/reference/slicing.md`: “Topologically sort the graph; publish in that order.” Also: “In this repo that convention (`docs/agents/backlog-policy.md` § Dependencies) is a **`- [ ] depends on #N` task-list line in the ticket body**; `run` treats a ticket with any unchecked, unclosed dependency as blocked and skips it.”
