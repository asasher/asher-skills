# niche ‚Äî candidate-market fit and where it lives

Position the person at the intersection of what they want, what the market wants, and how the market sees them ‚Äî sharpened into a hard-to-copy intersection, located in real communities. Requires the profile's strengths, market position, and constraints sections.

## Step 1 ‚Äî Draft the intersection

From the profile, generate two or three candidate niches: **[strength] √ó [domain they've lived] √ó [audience/beneficiary] √ó [format]** ‚Äî e.g., "data engineering √ó healthcare claims √ó mid-size payers √ó contracting."

Good niche criteria: complementary (the parts reinforce), useful (someone pays for it), specific (feels too narrow offline, viable at market scale), experiential (they can say "I've lived this"). Test each:
- "Where do people already ask for your take?"
- "What can you explain unusually well because you lived it?"
- "What tiny audience would immediately care?"

## Step 2 ‚Äî Reality-test as hypotheses, not identity

Each niche is a candidate-market-fit **hypothesis**: target roles/titles the market actually uses, the evidence you'd show, the likely objection, what would falsify it. Tested in the listening tour (`outreach`) ‚Äî market conversations decide, not introspection. Update after every ~5 conversations.

Two-pager discipline: condense to two pages ‚Äî situation, target hypotheses, constraints, evidence, open questions, specific asks ‚Äî to share with mentors, peers, or a job-search council. If the user has 2‚Äì3 peers in the same boat, suggest forming one: weekly check-in, shared commitments ‚Äî search morale and accountability are load-bearing.

## Step 3 ‚Äî Map the communities

For the leading niche (referrals and weak ties, not portals, carry most opportunity):
- Search patterns: `[role] Slack`, `[tool] Discord`, `[industry] community`, `[city] [role] meetup`, `[conference] speakers`, `[project] good first issue`, plus newsletters, subreddits, guilds, alumni groups.
- Verify each is alive: recent activity, real practitioners, a jobs/gigs channel.
- Pick **3‚Äì5** to join. Cadence: lurk 3‚Äì7 days to learn norms; answer 2‚Äì3 questions a week; share one genuinely useful artifact; ask for advice before ever asking for referrals. Contribution before extraction.

## Step 4 ‚Äî Decide the visibility strategy

Match ambition to the niche: for internet-native knowledge work, a proof-of-work cadence (one public artifact per week or two ‚Äî handled in `assets`) compounds luck. Where public building is unusual, visibility = speaking at the meetup, contributing to the guild, being known by the twenty people who matter. Don't prescribe an influencer path to someone whose market is twenty hiring managers.

## Output

Write `goodwork/NICHE.md`: intersection hypotheses with falsifiers, the two-pager, the community map with join/participation plan, the visibility strategy. Hand hypotheses to `outreach`, the proof-of-work plan to `assets`.
