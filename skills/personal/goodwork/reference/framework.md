# The Good Work Framework

The synthesis underneath every command. Read once per session, before the first command. Source list: the skill's `README.md`.

## What "good work" means here

Three senses at once, weighted per person, per season of life:

1. **Feels good** — engaging, energizing, produces flow.
2. **Is good** — meets a craft standard the person cares about; uses and grows their strongest skills.
3. **Does good** — serves someone or something the person endorses, within ethical lines they won't cross.

The person's own weighting of the three is part of the profile, not an assumption. Some want a calling; some want a well-paid job that funds a life. Both are good work. Never moralize.

## Advising priors

- **Interest match alone predicts satisfaction weakly.** The stronger predictors: work design (autonomy, variety, feedback, task significance), relationships, strength use, values fit, visible progress on meaningful work. Profile and weigh all of these.
- **Don't follow passion; follow evidence forward.** Passion usually develops from getting good at valuable work with autonomy and feedback. Persistent curiosity — what keeps tugging at attention — is a weak signal to test, not a verdict.

## The Good Work Profile (canonical schema)

Every command reads and writes `goodwork/PROFILE.md`. Sections:

1. **Snapshot** — what good work is for this person, in their own words; current status (employed/searching/redesigning); review date.
2. **Energy map** — energizers, drainers, anti-spark (competent but depleting), flow conditions (challenge level, feedback loop, interruption tolerance).
3. **Activities & interests** — preferred work activities (building/fixing, investigating, creating, helping, persuading/leading, organizing); persistent curiosities.
4. **Strengths & proof** — learned faster than peers, repeatedly asked for, recent episodes of unusually strong performance, overuse risks. Each strength needs at least one concrete episode.
5. **Anchors & values** — the one or two things they will not give up; top five values force-ranked; resolved tradeoffs (autonomy vs. security, pay vs. mission, novelty vs. predictability); dealbreakers. Evidence: what they actually chose when values conflicted.
6. **Motivation conditions** — which autonomy matters (goals, methods, schedule, tools, collaborators); what feedback makes them feel effective; what relatedness they need; what progress counts as a win; what makes a day feel wasted.
7. **Meaning & service** — who or what the work serves; calling status (searching / perceiving / living); ethical lines; what should stay unmonetized.
8. **Craft** — the craft they'd still refine after ten years; standards held when nobody is watching; mastery vs. novelty orientation.
9. **Market position** — career capital (rare-and-valuable skills, connections, credentials, reputation, runway); the hard-to-copy intersection; candidate-market fit hypotheses to test.
10. **Constraints & risk budget** — gravity constraints (accept/design around) vs. actionable ones; financial runway; visa/location/caregiving/health facts; decent-work floor (minimum pay, benefits, conditions).
11. **Whole-life dashboard** — work/play/love/health gauges and what work must not cost.
12. **Work orientation & weighting** — job / career / calling orientation; weighting of feels-good / is-good / does-good.

Confidence marks: label each claim **reported** (they said it), **evidenced** (backed by a story or observed choice), or **tested** (confirmed by a prototype or market feedback). Prototypes and the campaign exist to move items rightward.

## The arc

Define → Test → Position → Campaign → Sustain.

- **Define**: `interview` produces the profile; `odysseys` turns it into three alternative futures.
- **Test**: `prototype` converts futures into cheap experiments; `craft` redesigns the current job when leaving isn't wanted or possible yet.
- **Position**: `niche` fixes candidate-market fit and communities; `assets` builds CV, LinkedIn, and proof of work.
- **Campaign**: `targets` → `outreach` → `apply`, tracked in `pipeline`.
- **Sustain**: `daily` runs the action queue and journal; `review` closes the weekly loop and updates the profile.

People enter anywhere: mid-search can start at `targets` and backfill; burnt out starts at `interview` or `craft`.

## Workspace

One project folder per person. `goodwork/` holds operational JSON/JSONL (matchers, schedulers, approvals, UI projections) and narrative Markdown (the person, interview, weekly reflection). The state model, schemas, ID rules, writer rule, and evidence inbox live in [state.md](state.md). Create files on first use; update cumulatively. Contents are sensitive personal data: never share or publish without explicit instruction.
