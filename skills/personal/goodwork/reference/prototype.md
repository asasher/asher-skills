# prototype — test a work hypothesis cheaply

Identity changes through action, not introspection (Ibarra). A prototype turns "maybe I'd love X" into data. Each prototype tests **one named assumption** from the profile or an odyssey plan, as cheaply as possible.

## The ladder (escalate only when the cheaper rung passes)

1. **Read** (2–5 hours): the best insider accounts of the work — day-in-the-life posts, postmortems, salary threads, community archives. Kills fantasies fast and free.
2. **Conversations** (2–20 hours): talk to two or three people living a version of the path. This is reality sampling, not job begging — ask for their story, the daily reality, the tradeoffs, the entry paths, what they wish they'd known. Recruit via warm intros and weak ties; use the outreach mechanics in [outreach.md](outreach.md).
3. **Experience** (a day to four weeks): shadow someone, volunteer, take a short course, freelance one task, run a small project, attend the field's gathering, build a small artifact of the actual work.
4. **Small bet** (30–90 days, capped budget): a paid version — one client, one workshop, one product test. It only has to make its first $500 and teach something, not become a life purpose. Sell the service manually before building anything.

## Designing a prototype

For each, write down before starting:
- **Assumption tested** — one sentence ("I'd find grant-writing meaningful, not just prestigious").
- **The test** — activity, people, setting.
- **Success signal** — what you'd observe if the assumption is true (energy, quality of output, market pull), not just "I liked it."
- **Exit condition** — time box and spend cap. Experimentation without decision points becomes avoidance.

## Debrief (the part people skip)

After each prototype, capture: What did it teach about **energy** (engaged or performing engagement?), **competence** (fast learner here or grinding?), **credibility** (did insiders treat you as plausible?), **values** (anything violated?), and **market pull** (did anyone want more?). 

Then update the profile via `profile`: confirmed items move to *tested*; contradictions get revised, not rationalized. A failed prototype that saves a two-year mistake is a success — log it as such.

## Anti-patterns

- Testing three assumptions with one prototype (you won't know which one the result speaks to).
- The big-bang leap disguised as a prototype ("I'll just do the bootcamp/quit first").
- Serial prototyping with no decision point — set a review date where the odyssey gauges get re-scored.
- Only prototyping upward (rungs 3–4) when a conversation would have answered it.

## Output

Append to `goodwork/EXPERIMENTS.md`: one block per prototype (assumption, test, signal, exit, status, debrief). End each session with the single next prototype action, time-boxed, added to the `daily` queue.
