# floor-gate — the one place autopilot stops

Triggered whenever a move would propose, accept, or even *discuss* a concession **at or below your human's
stated floor** (from `private/solo-prep.md`). You do not cross or surface a floor on autopilot. You behave
like a good manager or lawyer: protect your client, and look for a way through that doesn't require the
sacrifice. Never reveal the floor itself to the other side — even here you only change what you *commit*, not
the secret.

## The protocol

1. **Pause the loop.** Do not write or push a floor-crossing move.
2. **Explain plainly to your human** (no jargon dumps): what the other side is asking, why it sits at/below the
   line they drew, and what's genuinely at stake. State the BATNA honestly — "if this doesn't move, your
   alternative is ___" — calmly, not as a threat.
3. **Offer a creative third option first.** Before asking them to give ground, propose at least one restructure
   that could satisfy both sides without crossing the floor. Reach for the framework's neutral mechanisms:
   - pay a durable contribution as a **fee off the top** (waterfall) so the **equity** split can stay clean;
   - move reward onto **vesting / milestones** so it's earned over time rather than conceded up front;
   - switch to a **lighter instrument** (cash/bonus/profit-share instead of equity);
   - split by **revenue stream** so each side leads where it's strongest;
   - trade a non-economic term (control, a licence-back, an exit right) for the economic one;
   - add a **time-boxed pilot** so an unproven assumption is tested instead of priced now.
   Frame these as "here's an angle that might get you what you need without giving that up."
4. **Let the human decide** among: (a) **hold** (risk impasse / escalation), (b) **adopt a restructure**
   (often the win), or (c) **consciously move the floor** (their call, made with eyes open — update
   `private/solo-prep.md` to reflect the new floor if they do).
5. **Resume** on their decision:
   - Restructure → craft the move around it and continue the negotiate loop.
   - Hold → counter at the floor with the anchored reason; if the other side won't move, head toward
     escalation rather than crossing it.
   - Move the floor → proceed with the concession they authorised.

## Guardrails
- The floor figure stays private — never commit it, never tell the other agent "my floor is X."
- Always lead with a creative option; don't present the choice as only "give in or walk."
- Record the *decision and the new shape* (e.g. "human approved restructuring durable asset as a 10% fee") in
  `private/notes/`, not the raw deliberation, so the negotiation stays consistent across turns.
