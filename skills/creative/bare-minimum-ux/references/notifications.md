Treat notifications as alarms.

1. Interrupt only when timely user action is required. If the required action is unnamed, route to a quiet surface.
2. Rationalize each notification class before shipping: action, urgency window, consequence if missed, sender, destination, quiet fallback, rate limit, expiry or batching, and suppression.
3. Keep non-actions out of interrupting channels; put status updates, reports, recommendations, marketing, and other FYI signals in activity feeds, logs, status panels, inboxes, or scheduled digests.
4. Do not give human messages, urgent actions, and FYI surfaces the same default visual treatment or sound.
5. Prevent floods and standing alarms: rate-limit senders, collapse repeated events, expire stale items, and avoid persistent badge counts without an obvious response.
6. Preserve credibility: remove, downgrade, or batch false positives, nuisance signals, stale badges, and ignored notifications; reduce interrupt rights for senders with low read or action rates.
7. Use scheduled summaries or user-chosen delivery windows for low urgency; do not make a blanket off switch the main design.
8. Never borrow urgent notification signals for engagement, reactivation, or sender goals that do not match receiver intent.
