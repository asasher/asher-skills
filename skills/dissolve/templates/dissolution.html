The question, as posed

empty

State it in the asker's own words. Then name the itch — what makes this feel like a real, hanging question rather than a settled fact. The itch is the thing we're dissolving; the confusion lives in the map, not the territory.

2

Taboo the words

empty

Ban the loaded word (free will, disease, fair, conscious, really) and re-ask using only what you actually mean. Where the substitution flows, the word was cheap. Where you cannot substitute without smuggling the word back — that spot is the confusion, now located.

Reduce to anticipated experience

empty

Turn each sub-question into what you'd actually expect to observe if it went one way versus the other. A sub-question whose two answers predict the same experience is empty — mark it dissolved-on-contact. The rest are real and may need evidence.

Resolution

empty

State the dissolution: with the word tabooed and the sub-questions separated, the original question is answered, empty, or replaced by sharper ones. Then run the gate — is there any lingering feeling of a hanging question? If yes, it is not dissolved; note where the pull remains and keep going.
