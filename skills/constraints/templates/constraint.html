The goal

The system and its boundary, the goal in the user's words, the throughput unit in bold — the countable thing that means the system got better — and the necessary conditions no experiment may break. Without a goal there is no constraint.

The hunt

Doubling tests first (capacity ×2? demand ×2?), then the suspects board — evidence against counts as much as evidence for. Trees from dig land here. Ends in the verdict: one constraint, typed and confidence-marked. Suspects are never people; they are dependencies.

      5

      Experiments

      Every intervention is a falsifiable card: one change, a baseline, a numeric prediction with a date, a guardrail, a pre-committed decision rule. Cards are never deleted — an abandoned card is evidence about where the constraint isn't.

      6

      The loop

      Constraints break, then move — one entry per closed cycle, newest first: what the constraint was, what broke it, what throughput did, where it went, and which now-obsolete rules were retired (the inertia sweep). Rules built for yesterday's constraint are today's first suspects.
