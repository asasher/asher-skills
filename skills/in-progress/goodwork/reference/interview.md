# interview — elicit what good work is for this person

Deliverable: `goodwork/PROFILE.md` in the schema from [framework.md](framework.md). A conversation, not a questionnaire — the schema is the coverage map, not the script.

## Mechanics (beyond the core rules)

- **Open from the record.** Play back what you inferred from the record and ask what you got wrong.
- **Switch to hypotheses early.** After the first few answers, offer a working read instead of open questions: "Sounds like you're more systems-builder than people-manager — right?" Corrections are denser than free recall.
- **Dependency order.** Career history and energy map before values tradeoffs; constraints before futures. Don't probe management appetite before knowing whether they want a leadership track at all.
- **Escape hatch.** Personal and fatigue-prone: offer breaks at act boundaries, and the short form up front if they're mid-search and urgent.

## Interview arc

### Act 1 — History
Walk the career chronologically, briefly. Per move: what pulled, what pushed, what they learned about themselves. Listen for the anchor — it shows in what they refused to give up, and in successful roles they still wanted to leave. Probes:
- "Which work moments felt most like *this is me at my best*? What exactly were you doing?"
- "Tell me about a role that was going well but still made you want to leave. What was missing?"
- "What attractive promotion or opportunity would you turn down? Why?"

### Act 2 — Energy
Collect three recent energized/absorbed episodes and three drained ones. For each: the actual activity, environment, interactions, tools, and beneficiaries. Then:
- "What are you good at that leaves you flat?" (anti-spark — the highest-yield question in the interview)
- "When do you lose track of time? What was the challenge level and the feedback loop?"
- "What are you excellent at but tired of being known for?" (excellence trap)
- "What keeps catching your attention lately — what do you keep saving, reading, discussing?" (weak signals)

### Act 3 — Values and conditions
Force tradeoffs; don't collect a wish list. "Choose: more autonomy or more security? More pay and status, or more mission? Novelty or predictability?" Verify against Act 1: "You chose mission — but in 2023 you took the higher-paying corporate role. What was true then?" Cover:
- Which autonomy actually matters: goals, methods, schedule, tools, collaborators.
- What feedback makes them feel effective; what progress counts as a win; what makes a busy day feel wasted.
- Relatedness: what collaboration energizes vs. drains; what they need from a manager and team.
- Dealbreakers: "What would make you leave a high-performing, well-paying team?"

### Act 4 — Meaning, craft, and weighting
- "Who or what should the work serve? Searching for a calling, aware of one, living one — or is that framing not you at all?"
- "What craft would you still want to refine after ten years? What standards do you hold when nobody's watching?"
- "What would still matter if it never became a job — and what should stay unmonetized?"
- "What lines won't you cross, whatever the pay?"
- Close: weight feels-good / is-good / does-good for this season of life.

### Act 5 — Constraints and whole life
- Runway, location, visa, caregiving, health, compensation floor. Classify each stated problem: control, influence, accept, or exit. Name gravity problems as design boundaries, without judgment.
- Work/play/love/health check: what is work currently costing that it must stop costing?
- "What career path would succeed professionally but damage the rest of your life?"

### Act 6 — Playback
Draft the snapshot in their own words and read it back section by section; they correct, you revise. Stop when every section has at least one evidenced entry and the user says the snapshot sounds like them — not merely impressive.

## Short form (urgent searchers)

One pass, ~10 questions: best-of episode, drained-despite-competent episode, anti-spark, forced value tradeoffs (3), dealbreaker, constraint scan, decent-work floor, weighting. Mark the profile `short-form`; backfill later via `profile`.

## Output

Write `goodwork/PROFILE.md` per the framework schema with confidence marks on every claim. Propose the next arc step: `odysseys` if direction is open, `craft` if they're staying put, `targets` if direction is settled and they need a job.
