# odysseys — three alternative five-year work futures

Generate real options, not the one true calling. Requires a profile; read `goodwork/PROFILE.md` first.

## Build three plans

Draft three genuinely different five-year paths **from the profile's evidence**, then refine with the user:

1. **Plan 1 — the current thing, done well.** The evolved version of the current path. Not a strawman: the best honest version.
2. **Plan 2 — the adjacent thing.** What they'd do if Plan 1 vanished tomorrow. Usually the anti-spark inversion plus the strongest under-used strength.
3. **Plan 3 — the unconstrained thing.** If money, image, and others' judgment didn't matter. Surfaces suppressed data; not automatically the "real" answer.

Per plan: a six-word title; a year 0–5 sketch (the work, the people, where the money comes from, one milestone per year); the two or three questions the plan would answer about their life; **gauges (1–5)**: Resources (time, money, skills, network), Excitement (honest affective pull), Confidence (belief they could test it), Coherence (fit with snapshot, values, whole-life dashboard).

## Rules

- Plans must diverge in kind, not salary band. Same daily activities in two plans → regenerate.
- Every plan names which energizers it feeds, which anchor it honors, which constraint it respects. A plan violating a gravity constraint is fantasy.
- At least one plan must fit the decent-work floor and risk budget *as they exist today*.
- Portfolio shapes count: three small bets can be a plan.
- Excellence-trap check: if all three extend what they're rewarded for but score low on excitement, force one plan from the "almost too easy to count" strengths.

## Choosing well (when they ask "so which one?")

1. **Gather** — plans, gauges, profile.
2. **Narrow** — the real choice is usually between two, or a hybrid.
3. **Choose** — best **doable** beats best theoretical. Gut reaction is data; coherence is the tiebreaker.
4. **Let go** — once chosen and tested, stop re-litigating. Decision quality is not outcome quality.

The next step after choosing is **never** "quit and leap" — it's `prototype`: each plan yields two or three cheap tests.

## Output

Write `goodwork/ODYSSEYS.md`: the three plans with gauges, the open questions each would answer, top prototype candidates per plan. Hand off to `prototype`.
