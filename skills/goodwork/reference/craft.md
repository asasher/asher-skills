# craft — redesign the current job before quitting it

"Don't resign, redesign" (Designing Your New Work Life) + Wrzesniewski's job crafting. For users who can't or don't want to leave yet — or who might not need to. Requires the profile's energy map and motivation conditions; run at least the short-form interview first.

## Step 1 — Diagnose before redesigning

- Map the current job as building blocks: the actual tasks, relationships, and where time/energy/attention go (not the job description).
- Score it against the profile: which energizers are present, which drainers dominate, which motivation conditions (autonomy kind, feedback, relatedness, progress) are unmet, which values are violated.
- **Sort the problems**: control / influence / accept (gravity) / exit. Be honest about the exit category: chronic values violation, indecent conditions, discrimination, harassment, or a manager who blocks all crafting are not crafting problems. Say so and route to `targets`.
- If the complaint is overwhelm, subtype it: **Hydra** (too many jobs in one), **Hyper** (pace), or **Happy** (overcommitted to good things) — the fixes differ: drop/delegate/renegotiate vs. pace boundaries vs. saying no.

## Step 2 — Craft along three axes

- **Task crafting**: expand the two tasks nearest the profile's energizers; shrink, batch, delegate, or renegotiate the top drainer. Concrete move: "What would you expand if your manager trusted you completely?" — then design the smallest visible win that earns that trust.
- **Relational crafting**: change who you interact with and how — join the project with the people who energize you, create a feedback loop with an actual user of your work, reduce surface area with energy sinks.
- **Cognitive crafting**: reframe who the work serves and what it's for — legitimate when it reconnects the job to a real beneficiary; illegitimate when it's anesthesia for a structural problem (check step 1's sort).

Also tune the **Maker Mix**: current vs. desired balance of money, impact, and expression this season — many "I hate my job" cases are a mix set wrong, not a wrong job.

## Step 3 — Run it as experiments

Each crafting move is a prototype: assumption, smallest version, success signal, two-week time box. Track in `goodwork/EXPERIMENTS.md` like any other prototype, and log energy in the journal so `review` can see whether the redesign is actually moving the needle.

Political reality check per move: whose approval does it need, whose work does it touch, and what does the org actually reward? Crafting that offloads your drainers onto a teammate or defies incentives will snap back.

## Step 4 — Test the exit before taking it

If two rounds of crafting move nothing, run "imagining the quit" as a prototype: draft the resignation conversation, price out the runway, and do a listening tour on the outside option — *before* resigning. If the exit still wins, quit well: reputation and relationships intact, and route to `targets` with the diagnosis as targeting data (you now know precisely what the next role must and must not contain).

## Output

A crafting plan appended to `goodwork/EXPERIMENTS.md` (moves, owners of approval, time boxes, signals), the problem sort recorded in the profile's constraints section, and one first move scheduled via `daily`.
