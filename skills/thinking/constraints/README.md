# Constraints

A stateful, user-involved Theory of Constraints facilitation: pin the goal, map the flow, hunt the constraint
down with evidence, exploit and subordinate before spending a cent, and run every intervention as a falsifiable
experiment — cycle after cycle, because the constraint moves. The whole state is one self-contained, visual
`constraint.html` per system: flow map, suspects board, logic trees, conflict clouds, experiment cards, and the
constraint history, all rendered inline and shareable as-is.

## Shape

- **Goal → Map → Find → Plan → Experiments → Loop** — six page sections, each with a done-when signal, driven
  by the Five Focusing Steps and the three thinking-process questions (what to change / to what / how).
- The page is the state (the `dissolve` pattern): `data-status` per section, a dashboard that answers "what's
  the constraint?" in one glance, FILL-marker regions, a session log, resumable across sessions.
- Facilitation style from `goodwork`: one hypothesis-anchored question at a time, evidence marks
  (suspected / evidenced / validated), opinions converted to observables before they're recorded.

## Sources

Synthesized from: Goldratt & Cox's *The Goal* (five focusing steps, throughput accounting, DBR); Goldratt's
*It's Not Luck* (thinking processes) and *Critical Chain*; Cox & Schleier's *Theory of Constraints Handbook*;
Dettmer's *The Logical Thinking Process*; Scheinkopf's *Thinking for a Change*; TOCICO body of knowledge
(UDEs, CRT, evaporating cloud, FRT/NBR, prerequisite/transition trees, categories of legitimate reservation,
three-cloud method); TOC Institute and LeanProduction practitioner guides; Kim, Behr & Spafford's *The Phoenix
Project* and the Three Ways; Anderson's kanban lineage and STATIK; Tendon's TameFlow (knowledge-work
constraints, management attention as constraint); Clarke Ching's *The Bottleneck Rules*; Deming's PDSA, Rother's
Improvement Kata, and Lean A3 for experiment discipline; Weisbord's six-box model as the organizational overlay.
Research notes live in `constraints-research/` at the repo root.

## Companions

When the constraint turns out to be a life/career question, hand off to `goodwork`. When a "constraint" is
really a confused question rather than a limited flow ("is our problem *culture*?"), `dissolve` it first.
