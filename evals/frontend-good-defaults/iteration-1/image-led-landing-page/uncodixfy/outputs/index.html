A Porto house with the volume turned down.

Rook House keeps the useful parts of a hotel close at hand: a staffed morning desk, proper beds, good coffee, and rooms that are calm after a day on the granite streets. The building keeps its old tilework and stair, then adds clean joinery, quiet lighting, and local ceramics without turning the place into a theme.
