## Core Mechanics

### 1. Workview And Lifeview

Purpose: build a “compass” before designing options. Workview surfaces beliefs about work; Lifeview surfaces beliefs about meaning. Coherence is the fit between who the person is, what they believe, and what they do.

Workview reflection prompt bank:
- Why work?
- What is work for?
- What makes work good, worthwhile, or meaningful?
- What role do money, status, security, growth, mastery, service, and fulfillment play?
- How should work relate to the individual, other people, and society?
- What is work not allowed to cost you?

Lifeview reflection prompt bank:
- Why are we here?
- What gives life meaning or purpose?
- What is the relationship between self, family, community, country, world?
- What counts as good, bad, just, unjust?
- Is spirituality, faith, nature, transcendence, or a larger order part of the picture?
- What roles do joy, sorrow, love, conflict, duty, peace, and contribution play?

Interview use: ask for concrete stories after each abstract answer. Example: “Tell me about a time work felt exactly like that.”

### 2. Good Time Journal And Wayfinding

Purpose: discover direction from lived evidence rather than abstract preference. The official Good Time Journal activity log has repeated activity-entry rows with gauges for engagement, energy, and a flow checkbox: [Good Time Journal PDF](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYL-Good-Time-Journal-Activity-Log-v21.pdf).

Tracks:
- Engagement: how absorbed/interested the person was.
- Energy: whether the activity left them more depleted or more alive.
- Flow: moments of deep immersion, time distortion, high attention.
- Repetition: daily entries over time, then pattern review.

AEIOU analysis:
- Activities: what was actually happening, not the job-title label.
- Environments: physical, digital, temporal, and social setting.
- Interactions: people, tools, systems, meetings, customers, managers.
- Objects: artifacts, software, documents, machines, materials.
- Users: who benefits, who participates, who evaluates, who is affected.

Energy-Engagement Map: the public worksheet asks users to list recurring energy-giving/draining activities, mark positive/negative energy effect, identify energy patterns, and name accessible changes: [Energy Engagement Worksheet](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYL-Energy-Engagement-Worksheet-v21.pdf).

### 3. Odyssey Plans

Purpose: generate multiple plausible lives, not choose the one “true” calling. Stanford describes Odyssey Plans as a way to explore curiosities and alternate futures: [Stanford resources](https://lifedesignlab.stanford.edu/resources).

Structure:
- Three alternative five-year plans.
- Plan 1: continuation or evolved version of the current path.
- Plan 2: what the person would do if Plan 1 became unavailable.
- Plan 3: what they would do if money, image, and judgment were not constraints.
- Each plan has a timeline from years 0-5, a six-word title, questions the plan addresses, and four gauges.

Dashboard gauges on the official worksheet:
- Resources: time, money, skills, credentials, network, access.
- I like it / likability: affective pull, excitement, desire.
- Confidence: belief they could test or pursue it.
- Coherence: fit with Workview/Lifeview and identity.
Source: [Odyssey Planning Worksheet](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYL-Odyssey-Planning-Worksheet-v21.pdf).

### 4. Prototyping Conversations And Experiences

Purpose: turn imagined futures into data. DYL treats prototypes as low-risk ways to ask questions, uncover assumptions, and create momentum; the official discussion questions identify prototyping as asking questions, uncovering hidden biases, iterating rapidly, and creating momentum: [DYL worksheets](https://designingyour.life/worksheets-and-discussion-questions/).

Conversation prototypes:
- Talk with someone living a version of the path.
- Ask for their story, daily reality, tradeoffs, entry path, surprises, and what they wish they had known.
- Do not treat it as job begging; treat it as reality sampling.

Experience prototypes:
- Shadow, volunteer, take a short class, run a small project, simulate a workday, freelance one task, attend a meeting/community, or build a small artifact.
- Good prototype tests one assumption cheaply.

### 5. Gravity Problems Vs Actionable Problems

Gravity problem: a fact or constraint that cannot be solved directly, only accepted, reframed, routed around, or used as a boundary condition. Bad framing: “How do I make gravity stop?” Better framing: “Given gravity, what can I design?”

The follow-up workbook operationalizes this as Minimum Actionable Problem work: write the problem, strip bias/drama/embedded solutions, reframe into “How might I/we…,” then brainstorm at least three prototypes: [DYWL Workbook PDF](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYWL-Workbook.pdf).

AI use: classify every complaint as control, influence, accept, or exit. Do not over-reframe structural harm as mindset.

### 6. Choosing Well

DYL’s decision process:
- Gather/create: generate real options through journaling, Odyssey Plans, conversations, prototypes.
- Narrow: reduce to a workable set; combine or eliminate options.
- Choose: use evidence, counsel, embodied reaction, and coherence.
- Let go: stop re-litigating after committing; separate decision quality from outcome quality.

The new-work workbook adds Best Doable Option vs Best Theoretical Option: discard fantasy-perfect options and act on the best available real option: [DYWL Workbook PDF](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYWL-Workbook.pdf).

## What Designing Your New Work Life Adds

Source: [official DYWL page](https://designingyour.life/books-designing-your-new-work-life/) and [DYWL workbook](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYWL-Workbook.pdf).

Adds a current-job redesign layer:
- “Don’t resign, redesign”: Reframe and Re-Enlist, Remodel, Reinvent, Relocate.
- Good Work Journal: daily reflection on learning, initiating, helping.
- Maker Mix: tune Money, Impact, Expression.
- Impact Map: compare roles by kind and reach of impact.
- Power-Sort: map influence/authority politics.
- Overwhelm diagnosis: Hydra, Happy, Hyper; then drop, delegate, renegotiate, reframe.
- Imagining Your Quit and Quitting Well: test leaving before leaving, then exit with reputation and relationships intact.
- Remote work, AI, and disruption: the official page positions the book for remote work, AI disruption, and continual redesign.

## Criticisms And Limitations

- Individualization risk: WIRED criticizes Designing Your Work Life for shifting responsibility for happiness onto workers while underplaying employer, economic, and institutional responsibility. Source: [WIRED](https://www.wired.com/story/being-happy-work-is-simply-not-enough).
- Privilege and access: prototyping assumes time, safety, network access, and optionality. People in precarious, discriminatory, visa-bound, caregiving-heavy, or low-control jobs may not be able to “design” much without collective or institutional change.
- Subjective gauges: engagement, energy, likability, confidence, coherence are useful heuristics, not validated psychometric scales.
- Recall bias: a one-time interview can distort patterns; the Good Time Journal is stronger when repeated over weeks.
- Reframing can be misused: gravity-problem acceptance is helpful for true constraints, harmful if it normalizes exploitation, harassment, burnout, or illegal conditions.
- Not therapy: the DYWL workbook explicitly flags burnout as a reason to seek specialized support rather than keep self-coaching. Source: [DYWL Workbook](https://designingyour.life/wp-content/uploads/2023/11/DYWL-Workbook.pdf).
- Odyssey Plans are option-generation, not forecasting. They need prototypes before major decisions.

## Best Translation Into AI One-On-One Interview

Best in a single interview:
- Workview/Lifeview elicitation: excellent for extracting a person’s definition of “good work.”
- Critical incident recall: ask for recent energizing/draining work episodes, then rate engagement, energy, flow.
- AEIOU probing: turns vague preferences into design requirements.
- Gravity/actionability sorting: prevents fake problem-solving.
- Odyssey sketching: generates alternative work identities quickly.
- Prototype planning: converts insight into next evidence-gathering action.
- Choosing well: useful once options exist; especially BDO vs BTO.

Better as longitudinal journaling:
- Good Time Journal.
- Energy-Engagement Map.
- Flow detection.
- Prototypes and follow-up learning.
- Letting-go/decision aftercare.

Weakest for AI-only:
- Life Design Team, peer feedback, and networked prototyping.
- Full visual mind mapping.
- Real-world power negotiation without human/legal/managerial context.

Recommended AI interview spine:
1. Current work dashboard.
2. Workview and Lifeview.
3. Three high-energy and three low-energy episodes.
4. AEIOU pattern extraction.
5. Definition of good work: tasks, environment, people, impact, autonomy, learning, money, boundaries.
6. Gravity vs actionable constraints.
7. Three Odyssey work futures.
8. Prototype conversations/experiences.
9. Best doable next step.
