# Career Ops Research Brief: Landing Work

## Best Combined Operating Model

Use **Bolles** for self-inventory, **Never Search Alone** for fit validation and emotional/accountability infrastructure, **2-Hour Job Search** for targeted outbound, then run the whole search as a **sales-style CRM pipeline** with weekly metrics, community participation, proof-of-work, and ATS-aware applications.

## 1. Phyl Terry: *Never Search Alone*

Sources: [Never Search Alone](https://www.neversearchalone.org/), [FAQ](https://www.neversearchalone.org/faq), [Join a JSC](https://www.neversearchalone.org/jsc), [Book page](https://www.neversearchalone.org/the-book)

**Core method:** five-step “Social Search”: form a Job Search Council, conduct a Listening Tour, set Candidate-Market Fit, network/interview, negotiate salary/budget/resources/support.

**Job Search Council mechanics:** 4-6 peer job seekers, similar career stage and search “speed” such as employed slow-seeker vs unemployed fast-seeker; volunteer moderator; regular Zoom; charter; weekly commitments; remain active through offer negotiation and pre-start planning. NSA says the method adds roughly **80 hours** of work and reports **5,400+ councils formed**.

**Candidate-Market Fit:** treat the candidate as the “product.” Before networking hard, identify the intersection of: what the candidate wants, what the market wants, and how the market sees the candidate. This is the best fit layer for an AI skill: turn profile data into hypotheses, then test them in market conversations.

**Mnookin two-pager:** public NSA pages mention it but do not expose the template. Implement it as a two-page decision memo: current situation, target hypotheses, constraints, evidence, open questions, and specific asks from the council. The point is not a resume; it is a structured artifact that lets peers challenge assumptions.

**Listening Tour mechanics:** before asking for referrals, ask for market truth. Suggested AI default: 15-25 conversations across former managers, peers, recruiters, hiring managers, role incumbents, niche experts, and community operators. Capture market language, repeated objections, skill gaps, target titles, salary reality, and “who else should I talk to?” Update CMF after every 5 calls.

**Effectiveness evidence:** NSA evidence is mostly operational and testimonial: thousands of councils, 20+ years of council practice, 300+ leader interviews for the book, 250+ testers, and thousands of job seekers supported. Strong external logic comes from job-search social support plus informational-interview evidence.

**Criticisms:** not randomized evidence; requires vulnerability and consistency; weak moderators or ghosting can break the system; upfront “slow down” work is hard for urgent searches; peer groups can reinforce bad assumptions unless the listening tour includes real hiring-market voices.

## 2. Steve Dalton: *The 2-Hour Job Search*

Sources: [2-Hour Job Search](https://2hourjobsearch.com/), [Resources](https://2hourjobsearch.com/resources), [LAMP PDF](https://2hourjobsearch.com/s/LAMP-Template.pdf), [“Why not the 1-Hour Job Search?”](https://2hourjobsearch.com/articles), [Contact2Colleague](https://www.contact2colleague.com/)

**Core method:** a time-boxed system for getting the first interview through targeted networking, not mass applications. Dalton says the first two hours should produce a **40-employer target list**, an outreach template, first contacts at the **Top 5** employers, and the first batch of outreach.

**LAMP list:** public template has 100 rows and columns for **Employer List, Advocacy, Motivation, Posting**. Older summaries often frame A as alumni/advocacy; the user’s “Alphabetize” is a sorting step, but the current public template labels the scoring column Advocacy. Operational flow: list 40 employers fast, alphabetize to avoid premature bias, score motivation, score availability of advocates/alumni/insiders, score live postings, sort into a Top 10.

**Top 10 targeting:** work the highest-ranked 10 companies as a queue; actively pursue contacts at the top 5 first; replace targets as they exhaust, go cold, or produce interviews.

**TIARA informational interview:** Trends, Insights, Advice, Resources, Assignments. Ask about market direction, company/role reality, what they would do in your position, what to read/join, and what small project or next step would prove readiness. Do not ask for a job first.

**3B7 follow-up:** after initial outreach, follow up after about **3 business days**, then again around **7 days** later; then stop or move to a lower-touch nurture. The point is pre-deciding follow-up so anxiety does not drive random checking.

**Template skeleton:**  
`Subject: Quick question from [shared context]`  
`Hi [Name] - I’m researching [role/field] and noticed your work on [specific reason]. Could I ask for 20 minutes about your path and what you’re seeing in the market? I’m not asking for a job; I’m trying to learn how to focus my search.`

**Effectiveness evidence:** Dalton’s public claim is process discipline, not a universal placement statistic. The mechanism is supported by referral/networking evidence and BLS informational-interview guidance. Strongest use case: white-collar, network-visible roles where insiders can explain demand and route candidates.

**Criticisms:** alumni/LinkedIn access favors people with credentialed networks; less suited to union, civil-service, hourly, or highly regulated hiring; cold outreach can be spammy if over-automated; evidence is practitioner/career-center based rather than broad causal labor-market proof.

## 3. Richard Bolles: *What Color Is Your Parachute?*

Sources: [Official Parachute site](https://parachutebook.com/), [BLS informational interviewing PDF](https://www.bls.gov/careeroutlook/2002/summer/art03.pdf), [Book overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/What_Color_Is_Your_Parachute)

**Flower Exercise:** seven-petal self-inventory used to define target work: favorite transferable skills, favorite fields/knowledge, preferred people, preferred working conditions, preferred salary/responsibility, preferred geography, and purpose/values. For an AI skill, convert petals into structured search filters and disqualifiers.

**Bolles’ job-hunting method ranking:** editions vary, but the classic table argues that low-yield methods include internet/posting-only search, blind resumes, ads, and agencies; higher-yield methods involve personal contacts, direct employer approaches, and targeted conversations with people who can hire. Commonly cited Bolles ranges: online/posting-only roughly low single digits to low teens; private agencies 5-28%; asking contacts for leads about 33%; direct employer contact around 47%; highly targeted self-inventory plus direct approach up to 80%+. Treat these as legacy heuristic data, not modern causal evidence.

**Mechanics:** do self-inventory first, identify specific kinds of organizations and problems, talk directly to people doing or hiring that work, then tailor resume/interview language around the candidate’s strongest transferable skills.

**Effectiveness evidence:** Parachute’s durability is strong: 50+ years, 10M+ copies, annual revisions, widespread career-center adoption. The BLS article credits Bolles with coining informational interviewing and recommends 20-30 minute conversations, research beforehand, about 10 prepared questions, referral asks, and thank-you notes within a few days.

**Criticisms:** the method can over-index on introspection; old success-rate tables predate modern ATS, LinkedIn, remote work, and AI-saturated applications; assumes the seeker has enough time, agency, and choice to optimize for fit.

## 4. Modern Practice

Sources: [WSJ networking/referrals](https://www.wsj.com/lifestyle/careers/networking-job-search-c6f06b0c), [Business Insider hidden market](https://www.businessinsider.com/hidden-job-market-rejection-offer-2025-8), [WSJ ghost jobs](https://www.wsj.com/lifestyle/careers/ghost-jobs-2c0dcd4e), [Ghost jobs arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.21771), [iCIMS referral survey via Glamour](https://www.glamour.com/story/reference-job-important), [Weak ties / Science coverage](https://www.axios.com/2022/09/28/weak-ties-career-mobility-jobs-linkedin-data), [Science weak-ties paper](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abl4476)

**Hidden job market:** the sloppy “80% of jobs are hidden” claim is overused. Better model: many roles are public, but winners often come through referrals, prior candidate pools, recruiter memory, internal transfers, stealth openings, executive searches, or small-company word of mouth. BI quotes recruiters saying most roles are posted, but prior applicants and referrals can be fast-tracked.

**Referral evidence:** WSJ reports recruiters leaning heavily on referrals as application volume rises; one recruiter estimated only 40% of successful applicants came cold through the portal. PayScale reportedly doubled referral share to 30%, while its 53,000-worker survey found referral systems can advantage white men. iCIMS’ older vendor survey found employers highly value referrals. Net: referrals are powerful but inequitable.

**Weak ties:** Granovetter’s classic finding and a later LinkedIn/Science study both support outreach beyond close friends. Moderately weak ties can carry fresher job information than close contacts.

**ATS-aware resumes:** use standard headings, role-matched keywords, exact tools, measurable outcomes, no keyword stuffing, no fake skills, and simple formatting unless the employer requests otherwise. A 2026 resume-tailoring case study found fit-score gains when the candidate had relevant stored evidence, but worse scores when domain overlap was weak: AI tailoring must be evidence-gated. Source: [ATS overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Applicant_tracking_system), [resume-tailoring arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.05257)

**LinkedIn optimization:** headline should describe target professional identity, not “open to work”; use target-role keywords, complete experience, proof links, recommendations, and a clear About section. Time and Investopedia summarize recruiter-visible profile factors: photo, headline, career history, connections, education, recommendations. Sources: [Time](https://time.com/4403286/linkedin-tips/), [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/personal-finance/012915/how-use-linkedin-get-job.asp)

**Proof-of-work:** strongest for roles where work artifacts are inspectable: engineering PRs, product teardowns, design case studies, GTM plans, analysis notebooks, writing samples, community talks. A 2025 open-source resume study found hiring managers value OSS contributions as evidence of initiative and non-technical traits, but only with sustained, relevant contribution. Source: [Open Source Resume](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25180)

## 5. Daily-Action / Cadence System

Sources: [2HJS cadence article](https://2hourjobsearch.com/articles), [BI sales-cycle job search](https://www.businessinsider.com/treated-job-like-sales-cycle-landed-new-role-two-months-2025-4)

**Pipeline fields:** target company, target role, fit score, source, insider contacts, last touch, next action, application status, referral status, interview stage, objections, notes, proof artifact, probability, close/loss reason.

**Default weekly quotas:** 20 new targets until 100-150 total; 10-25 high-quality outreach messages depending on search urgency; 3-5 informational calls; 5-10 high-fit applications; 2-3 follow-up blocks; 1 proof artifact per strategic niche every 1-2 weeks.

**Follow-up cadence:** initial outreach, 3 business-day follow-up, 7-day follow-up, then archive or nurture monthly. After conversations: thank-you within 24-72 hours, send promised resource, ask permission before using names, update them on outcomes.

**Metrics:** outreach response rate, call-booking rate, referral conversion, application-to-screen rate, screen-to-onsite rate, onsite-to-offer rate, time-to-response, source-of-interview, target-fit score, energy/confidence score, and reasons for rejection.

**Criticism:** quotas can become vanity metrics. The AI should optimize for interviews per high-fit target, not raw applications. Over-automation damages trust.

## 6. Communities and Niches

Sources: [BI job seekers teaming up](https://www.businessinsider.com/how-tech-job-seekers-teaming-up-find-work-network-market-2026-1), [Open Source Resume](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25180), [OSS overview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open-source_software_movement)

**Discovery mechanics:** search by role, tool, niche, conference, newsletter, podcast, subreddit, GitHub org, meetup, Slack, Discord, alumni group, professional guild, certification body, and creator/community operator. Query patterns: `[role] Slack`, `[tool] Discord`, `[industry] community`, `[city] [role] meetup`, `[conference] speakers`, `[open source project] good first issue`.

**Participation cadence:** join 3-5 relevant communities; lurk 3-7 days; map norms and recurring names; answer 2-3 questions weekly; share one useful artifact; do mock interviews or reviews; ask for advice before referrals; only ask for referral after a real interaction.

**Routes into work:** community job boards, “who’s hiring” channels, maintainer networks, mock interview groups, conference speaker backchannels, open-source issues, local meetups, niche newsletters, and warm intros from peers.

**Evidence:** BI reported job seekers using Slack/community groups for mock interviews, leads, confidence, and referrals; one candidate did about 40 mock interviews in six weeks through a large private Slack before landing. OSS evidence supports visible contributions as hiring signal, especially for initiative and collaboration.

**Criticisms:** communities can be noisy, exclusionary, or unpaid-labor traps; access is uneven; many groups discourage transactional job asks; proof-of-work can favor people with spare time.

## AI Career Ops Skill Design Implications

Build around four loops: **fit loop** from Flower + CMF, **market loop** from listening tours, **pipeline loop** from LAMP/CRM, and **proof loop** from applications, interviews, communities, and artifacts.

The skill should generate: target list, ranked Top 10, outreach drafts, 3B7 reminders, TIARA question sets, ATS-aware resumes from verified evidence, LinkedIn/profile edits, proof-of-work briefs, community maps, daily action queues, and weekly metric reviews.
